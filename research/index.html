Research

Our team is trying to understand why some animals can regenerate tissue, organs, or entire body parts, while others cannot. To do this, we compare highly regenerative organisms, such as flatworms and spiny mice (Acomys), with their close relatives, which can only heal wounds by forming scars. We also want to increasingly establish in vitro methods, such as induced pluripotent stem cell technology (iPSC) for the production of tissue cultures, to supplement and partially replace our in vivo regeneration studies. Our goal is to find ways to improve human regenerative capacity in the future.

Research Areas

We aim to uncover how physiological systems enable resilient animal function across environments. Our near‑term focus is on integrative, multi‑scale measurements, advanced imaging and data‑driven modeling to link mechanisms from cells to behavior. We will strengthen open data practices and interdisciplinary collaborations to accelerate discovery and translation.
